Kerala: One more Shigella death, seven fresh cases reported



Kerala has reported one more death due to Shigella infection along with seven new cases, raising concerns among health authorities across the state. The latest victim was a 54-year-old woman from Kozhikode district who was undergoing treatment after testing positive for the bacterial infection. With this death, the total number of Shigella-related deaths reported in Kerala during June 2026 has increased. Health officials are closely monitoring the situation and have intensified surveillance activities in affected areas to prevent further spread of the disease. The government has also urged hospitals and healthcare workers to remain alert and report suspected cases immediately. Shigella is a highly contagious bacterial infection that affects the digestive system and commonly spreads through contaminated food, water, or poor hygiene practices. Symptoms include diarrhea, fever, stomach pain, nausea, and vomiting, with severe cases leading to dehydration and other complications. In response to the recent cases, health authorities have advised the public to maintain proper hand hygiene, consume safe drinking water, and ensure that food is prepared and stored hygienically. Public awareness campaigns are also being conducted to educate people about preventive measures. Officials believe that strict adherence to hygiene and sanitation practices can help control the spread of the infection and protect public health.
