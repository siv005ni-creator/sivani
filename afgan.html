Afghanistan launches construction work on major higher education projects



The Ministry of Higher Education in Afghanistan has officially launched construction work on five major higher education infrastructure projects across different provinces of the country. According to reports, these projects are aimed at improving educational facilities and creating a better learning environment for students and faculty members. The initiative reflects the government's efforts to strengthen the higher education sector by expanding access to quality educational resources and modern infrastructure. The new projects are expected to include academic buildings, classrooms, administrative facilities, and other essential structures needed for universities and higher education institutions. The development of these infrastructure projects is considered an important step toward supporting Afghanistan's educational growth and human resource development. By investing in higher education, the government hopes to provide students with improved opportunities for learning, research, and professional development. The projects are also expected to create employment opportunities during the construction phase and contribute to the social and economic development of the regions where they are being built. Once completed, these facilities will help accommodate a larger number of students and enhance the overall quality of higher education in Afghanistan.
